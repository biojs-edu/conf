Suzi Lewis

Berkeley Lab, Berkeley, California, US

Her group is focused on the development, use, and integration of ontologies into biological data analysis. She is interested in the management of large-scale biological data: describing it rigorously using ontologies; designing databases that are flexible enough to be easily maintained; quickly searching and accurately retrieving data; and presenting it effectively to researchers and biologists. She believe that the only way to develop effective software is through user-centered design. In broad terms, user-centered design (UCD) is a design philosophy and a process in which the needs, wants, and limitations of the end user of an interface or document are given extensive attention at each stage of the design process. Members of her group contribute to a number of projects, including the Gene Ontology, the National Center for Biomedical Ontology, modENCODE, and the Generic Model Organism Database Project.

Industry Session

Friday 3 July




The BioJS Community have partnered with Repositive.io, an SME based in Cambridge, UK, to understand the needs, wants, and limitations of BioJS interfaces applied to industry-based end users. We will be including representatives from a variety of SMEs, big pharma and profit organisations.




Lightning Talks

Friday 3 July




Share your thoughts with the BioJS community!

Lightning talks are different from usual talks. A maximum of 5 minutes per talk is allowed so only one single focused topic can be addressed.
You have this great idea? Or you want to talk about your past experience with BioJS? Maybe you even have some concerns about something BioJS related you’d like to bring up? Then you should give a lightning talk!
You can use powerpoint slides or a video - it doesn’t matter! What counts is that your voice is heard.






Hackathon: Pizza & Code

Saturday 4 July




Become a top BioJS contributor!

Join expert in the field of science, data visualisation, JavaScript programming as well as industry partners and help develop applications that matter. We want to take BioJS to the next level and you can help us reach that goal.

In the beginning of the Hackathon a number of objectives will be set and we will hack together to make them become reality. An abundance of pizza and other snacks will be available to fuel your bodies and provide the energy you need to work your magic.
